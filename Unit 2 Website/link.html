How do these goals link?

Human activities are changing the Earth's natural atmosphere. Over the last century the burning of fossil fuels like coal and oil
has increased the concentration of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. This happens because the coal or oil burning process
combines carbon with oxygen in the air to make CO2. To a lesser extent, the clearing of land for agriculture, industry,
and other human activities has increased concentrations of greenhouse gases as well.

In its Fifth Assessment Report, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, a group of 1,300 independent scientific experts
from UN member countries all over the world concluded there's a more than 95% probability that human activities over the past
50 years have warmed our planet.

The industrial activities that society depends upon have raised atmospheric carbon dioxide levels from 280 to 400 parts per million
in the last 150 years. The panel also concluded there's a more than 95% probability that human-produced greenhouse gases
such as carbon dioxide, methane and nitrous oxide have caused much of the observed increase in Earth's temperatures over the past 50 years.

The effort to switch to using clean, renewable energy is not only beneficial for the environment, but also society as well. Besides
the environmental benefits, solar and wind power sources don't run out, while non-renewables such as coal or oil do. The sun
and wind are everywhere, so there is no need for wars over a resource used as power. The environmental benefits of using
clean energy however are greater, as one of, if not the biggest contributor to increased atmospheric CO2 levels is coal
and oil power plants. Switching to using clean, renewable energy instead of relying on power plants which produce smog and
CO2 is a clean and simple way to get energy without hurting the enivronment.

The Paris Agreement is a good first step, but getting governments and big corporations to actually stop using fossil fuels and
to start using renewable, clean energy is what needs to happen next if we want to stay behind the point of no return. It may be
difficult, but if enough people speak up and take action, it could be an attainable goal.
